What is ESG?

Environmental, Social, and Corporate Governance (ESG) factors are used to measure the sustainability and societal impact of an investment in a company or business. The analysis of the three criteria provides an indication of the future financial performance of a company, including return and risk. Stakeholders are interested in the ESG performance of an organization to see if it adheres by sustainability and social responsibility principles, and if the company is accurately identifying and addressing risks associated with climate change.

SEBI's BRSR ESG Reporting format

In India ESG reporting is known as Business Responsibility and Sustainability Reporting. The Government agency running it is SEBI (Securities and Exchange Board of India). There is a guideline/ questionnaire prepared by SEBI for reporting ESG/BRSR.

Section A

Deals with the General Disclosures which includes the basic information about the company. Section A includes information, particularly; size, location, products, number of employees and CSR activities undertaken by the company. Section B

Section B includes information relating to Management and Process of the company. . The purpose of this section is to understand whether the company has the building blocks in place which enable and ensure responsible business conduct. The company is required to disclose information regarding its policies and processes relating to leadership, governance and stakeholder engagement.

Section C

Section C includes information related to Principle-wise performance of the company. This section indicates how a company is performing in respect of each principle and core elements of responsible business conduct. The questions in this section are divided into 2 parts, namely; Essential and leadership . The questions of essential categories are mandatory and the ones of leadership are voluntary to answer for the companies.
